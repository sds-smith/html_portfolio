Whole-Brain Thinker

What I love most about building software is that it equally requires a logical, analytical mind and a creative, divergent imagination.

It pushes us into the space between the rigid constraints of a compiler and the infinite possibilities of a blank canvas.

For me, it's a medium for linear-creative expression, where the "how" of technical architecture meets the "why" of the human goal.

Ever Curious

Through her entire life, my grandmother viewed the world with the wide-eyed wonder of a child.

No matter how knowledgeable she became, she never lost the humility to ask "why?" or "what if?"

Maybe this has inspired my own lifelong curiosity, as I approach each new challenge with those very same questions.

Human Centered

Software is about people - those who use it and those who develop and maintain it. As a Software Engineer, I put people first by listening to understand before jumping into a solution.

My time in operations gave me the skills necessary for ensuring technical solutions always serve the human goal.
